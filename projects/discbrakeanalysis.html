Design and Analysis of Automobile Disc Brake

  • March - May 2016/a>

Project Info

Mechanical Design for High Temperature Course Project (2016)
Advisor â€“ Prof. Nasr Ghoniem (Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, University of California Los Angeles)

The objective of this project is to analyze the operation of a disc brake under high temperature. Disc brake is also called floating caliper brake, has a floating caliper, which supports a single floating piston that is actuated by hydraulic pressure. Actuations of the pistons forces the friction pad to clamp on the brake disc during braking operation, which causes a temperature rise on the disc surface.
Porsche 911 Carrera S has been selected for this study. For this specific vehicle, the frontal brake has three pistons while the rear one has two. The vehicle weights 1605kg and has a top speed of about 305km/hr.A normal condition is considered when the vehicle is running at 120km/hr with braking at intervals of 50s. Off-normal condition is when vehicle is running at its maximum speed of about 300km/hr with a single hard braking. A multiphysics approach including mechanical stresses, temperature effect and air flow is considered to study the reliability of the brake design.
